MAAV Melrose Alliance Against Violence

The Melrose Alliance Against Violence (MAAV for short) is a community-based agency committed to ending domestic violence. We believe that no one deserves to be abused or to live in fear. We work to end domestic violence and promote healthy relationships by increasing community awareness, educating young people in the schools, training teachers, coaches and parents, and helping victims of domestic violence, teen dating violence and bullying. Our goal is to stop violence in relationships before it starts.

Student Action Board

The Student Action Board (SAB) is a dynamic group of 20 peer leaders at Melrose High School who work in the schools and community to promote respect and prevent abuse in relationships. Students can apply to the SAB at the end of 9th grade. The SAB educates their peers on topics like the warning signs of abuse, consent in relationships and how to help a friend. They are involved in poster campaigns to raise awareness and promote bystander action, MAAV’s Annual Walk, and a program called “Escalation” for the senior class, among other things!

Annual Walk & Candlelight Vigil

Each October, MAAV sponsors an Annual Walk & Candlelight Vigil, “bringing to light” the problem of domestic violence, commemorating the lives of victims and survivors of domestic violence from the previous year, and uniting the community in taking a stand against violence. The Walk is MAAV’s signature event and involves over 900 people of all ages.

MAAV Community Coffeehouse

2014 marked the inception of the Community Coffeehouse, Voices Against Violence. The vision came from two high school students who wanted to use the power of music and the arts to spread the message of healthy relationships and create discussion about teen dating violence. Featuring teens and adult performers, the popular Community Coffee House has become an annual event that combines the talents of the people of Melrose to spread an important message.

White Ribbon Day

White Ribbon Day started in Canada in response to the massacre of 14 women by a man at a technical college. Today, the International White Ribbon Campaign has spread to over 60 countries. The purpose of the campaign is for men to take a stand against gender-based violence, and to promote healthy masculinity and respect in relationships. In Melrose, we hold a ceremony in the community, an assembly at Melrose High School, and raise the White Ribbon Day flag as we promise to part of the solution in building a safe community where everyone thrives and can be their whole selves.

Melrose Run for Women

Every year on Mother’s Day, the Melrose Running Club sponsors the Melrose Run for Women and donates the proceeds to MAAV. This 5K race starts and ends at Pine Banks Park in Melrose, and attracts women, teens and children of all ages. The event also includes prizes, fun activities for kids, refreshments and music, and is a great Mother’s Day tradition for many families.
