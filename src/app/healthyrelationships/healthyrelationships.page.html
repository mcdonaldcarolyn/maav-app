Tips for breaking up safely

1. If you don't feel safe, don't break up in person. It may seem cruel to break up over the phone or by text but it may be the safest way. 2. If you break up in person, so it in a public place. Have a friend or a parent wait nearby. Have your cell phone with you. 3. Don't try to explain your reasons for ending the relationship more than once. There is nothing you can say that will make your ex-partner happy. 4. Let your friends and parents know you are ending your relationship, especially if you think your ex-partner will come to your house or confront you when you're alone. 5. If your ex-partner does come to your house when you're alone, don't go to the door. 6. Trust yourself. If you feel afraid, you probably have a good reason. 7. Ask for help. Go to www.loveisrespect.org and chat with a trained peer advocate who is able to answer your questions. Talk to any trusted adult or call MAAV at 781-662-2010. Just because an unhealthy or abusive relationship is over, that doesn't mean the risk or violence is too. Talk with your friends, family, and trusted adult at school so they can support you.
